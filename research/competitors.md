**The graveyard is the lesson.** Every channel that re-clips one official podcast
without adding its own layer is dead — 20 to 613 subs even after years and hundreds
of uploads. "Stick Football" started last month doing exactly what we do and has 0
subs and ~6 views/video. **We are currently structured like the graveyard, not like
the winners.** We even clip the *same episodes* The Overlap clips itself: their Roy
Keane "don't underestimate Mexico" short has **1.27M views**; our clip from the same
episode has 3. The algorithm will never serve a copy over the official source with
1.7M subs posting 50×/month.

## What the winners share (measured, not vibes)

1. **A recognizable NAME in nearly every title** — Roy Keane, Dana White, McGregor,
   Ronaldo. Names carry search + instant recognition in the feed.
2. **Emotional verb or question titles**: "X HUMBLED Y!", "X SHOCKS THE WORLD!",
   "Roy warns England…", Tifo's pure-question format ("What's the biggest World Cup
   upset of all time?" — 533k). Questions double as comment bait.
3. **Deliberate emoji** (Full Send prefixes 😱💰; Overlap suffixes 🇲🇽👀).
4. **Cadence**: 16–50 uploads/30d. (Our 3/day = 90/30d is already top-tier — keep.)
5. **Median Short length 25–57s** (Overlap 40s, Full Send 25.5s, Rest Is Football
   34s). Our 20–47s is in range — length is not our problem.
6. **Shorts are the funnel, LONG-FORM is the business**: Overlap sample 21 shorts :
   26 longs; Tifo 7:27. The big channels earn on long-form watch hours; Shorts
   recruit. A Shorts-only channel has no monetization flywheel.

## Algorithm reality for a channel our size (current, 2026)

- Every Short is cold-seeded to a **test pool of ~50–500 viewers**; a brand-new
  channel seeing **10–30 first-day views is normal** — our 2–3 means we're failing
  even the seed test (swipe-aways) *and* under-seeded (new channel, no niche signal).
- The gate metric is **watch-time-per-impression / swipe-through in the first 3
  seconds**; Shorts swiped in <3s get capped regardless of anything else.
- **~70% average retention** is the rough "good" threshold; a 35s Short clears the
  watch-time gate at ~65%.
- **Loop rate** (rewatches >100%) is a ranking signal — endings that snap back into
  the opening earn free retention.
- Niche coherence matters for *who* the seed pool is: a channel that posts football
  + creatine + street racing gives the system no stable audience to seed to.

## Monetization rules that shape the long-form plan (current, 2026)

- Compilations/clips ARE monetizable **only with significant original value**:
  editing that tells a story, genuine analysis, educational framing — "creative
  ownership," not editing effort.
- **Voiceover/on-screen commentary under ~30% of runtime triggers review.** Design
  target: ≥30% narrator/analysis share in every compilation.
- **AI narration alone does not make reused content original** — the narration must
  carry real editorial structure (thesis, analysis, ranking, payoff).
- "Inauthentic content" policy blocks mass-produced/templated videos.
- YPP 2026: apply at **500 subs + 3,000 watch hours/90d (or 3M Shorts views/90d)**;
  full ad revenue at 1,000 subs + 4,000 watch hours/12mo. Long-form watch hours are
  the realistic path — 3M Shorts views is not.

## Strategic conclusion (what actually changes)

1. **Stop being a mirror; become a curator.** Our unfair advantage vs official
   channels: we clip ACROSS shows (Overlap + Rio + Rogan + DOAC). No official
   channel can do cross-podcast curation. Every piece of content gets an editorial
   angle: a take, a ranking, a question — never just "here's what Roy said."
2. **Launch themed long-form compilations** (weekly): "Every pundit is wrong about
   France — here's the evidence from 4 podcasts" beats "Roy Keane clip #47". This
   is simultaneously the differentiation play AND the monetization play (watch
   hours) AND policy-safe (≥30% narrator analysis).
3. **Win the 3-second gate**: first frame = name + stake burned in; ending loops.
4. **Titles**: NAME + emotional verb/question, front-loaded, one emoji.
5. **Niche-lock while the World Cup runs** (through ~Jul 19): football drama only —
   give the algorithm ONE audience to seed. Revisit after the final.
