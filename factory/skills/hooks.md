# Skill: Hooks

The first 1–3 seconds. A clip lives or dies here.

**Proven hook patterns** (pick the one the moment naturally fits — never force one):
- **Bold claim:** "Most people save money completely wrong."
- **Curiosity gap:** "Nobody tells you what actually happens after you quit."
- **Stakes/number:** "I lost $40,000 learning this one lesson."
- **Contrarian:** "Hard work is overrated. Here's what beats it."
- **Story cold-open:** drop straight into the tense moment — "So he pulls out the gun…"
- **Callout / relatability:** "If you're under 30 and broke, watch this."
- **Question:** "Why do the smartest people you know seem the least busy?"

**Rules:**
- Lead with the payoff line, not the windup. Cut the "well, you know, I think that…" preamble.
- The on-screen text hook and the spoken hook should reinforce, not duplicate, each other.
- Front-load motion or a strong word in the first frame.
- If the strongest line is 10s in, START the clip there and let context come after.
- Avoid generic openers: "In this video…", "Today I want to talk about…", greetings.
