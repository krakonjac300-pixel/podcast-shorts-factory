# Skill: Short-Form Strategy

How vertical short-form (Shorts / Reels / TikTok) actually wins.

- **Only two signals earn distribution (2026):** swipe-away rate (did they stay past ~3s)
  and average view duration plus rewatches. Completed views and rewatches now count far more
  than impressions. Likes, subs, and shares are downstream outcomes, not levers.
- **Benchmarks to beat:** under-30s clips want 55%+ average view duration, 30-60s want 45%+.
  "Stayed to watch" (did not swipe in ~3s) should sit at 70%+. AVD above ~80% of length is
  the expansion trigger beyond the test pool; under ~60% reach stalls.
- **The first 3 seconds decide 80% of the outcome.** A 30-50% drop in 0-3s is normal; the job
  is to beat it. Open on the most surprising line, not the setup. Seconds 3-10 are the
  commitment phase and lose another 15-25% if the payoff drags.
- **Read the retention curve like a diagnosis, not a score:** cliff at 0-3s = weak first
  frame, cut the open harder; drop at 3-10s = payoff delayed, halve the setup; a dip at one
  timestamp = that exact beat is broken, cut it; a mid-video spike = a rewatched moment,
  clone whatever caused it; a flat plateau above 70% = the winner shape, make more of it.
- **Self-contained.** A clip must make complete sense with zero outside context. Never start
  mid-reference ("like I said earlier...").
- **One idea per clip.** Don't bundle. One claim, one story, one payoff.
- **End on a loop or a question** so viewers rewatch or comment. Avoid limp endings ("...so
  yeah"). End AT the payoff; every second after it costs retention.
- **Ideal length:** 18-40s for talking-head clips. Go to 60s only if the story earns it.
- **Comment bait:** controversial-but-defensible takes, "agree?" moments, and small factual
  hooks people love to correct all drive the comments that boost reach.
- **Caption/title does half the work:** it sets the expectation the hook then pays off.
