# Skill: Trending Audio & Music

Sound choice is a discovery lever, not just vibe — especially on TikTok and Reels.

- **Trending sounds boost reach.** TikTok/Reels surface content using a rising sound to people
  who engaged with that sound. Riding a relevant trending audio can multiply impressions.
- **Only if it fits.** Never bury the speaker or undercut the message for a trend. For
  talking-head clips the voice IS the audio — a trending track sits *under* it, quiet, or only
  fills the intro/outro. Forced trends feel cheap and hurt retention.
- **How to use under dialogue:** keep music at ~8–15% so speech stays crisp; bring it up only in
  pauses, the cold-open, or the last beat.
- **Match emotion to mood:** tense underscore for stories, upbeat for energy/listicles, lo-fi
  or silence for intimate confessions. Mood beats popularity.
- **Originality also wins:** a distinctive, consistent sound signature (a recurring intro sting)
  builds recognition across a series.
- **Licensing:** use platform-native sound libraries (in-app) or cleared royalty-free tracks.
  Commercial/copyrighted music can mute audio or block the video, killing reach.
- **Practical pipeline note:** the editor mixes a local royalty-free track (ducked under voice)
  and records the recommended *mood*; swap in a platform-native trending sound at upload time
  in-app when it fits the clip.
