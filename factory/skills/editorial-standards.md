# Editorial Standards — the channel's quality bar

Written from a real frame-by-frame review of our published clips (2026-07-03).
Every agent applies the part that touches its job. The Manager uses this as the
pre-upload review checklist — a clip that violates a MUST gets bounced back.

## FINDER — what makes a clip worth cutting
- MUST: the moment works as pure audio+face. AVOID segments where hosts are
  watching/reacting to another video or screen ("look at this", "watch this",
  "this video", "did you see") — screen-recording layouts crop terribly to
  vertical and add third-party copyright on top of the podcast's.
- MUST: the first sentence works as a cold open (curiosity or bold claim).
- PREFER: specific numbers, named entities, contrarian claims, stakes.
- PREFER: 25–50s. One idea per clip — no topic changes mid-clip.

## EDITOR — how it must look and sound
- MUST: nobody half-cropped. 2+ faces → full-frame fit; face smaller than ~2%
  of the frame = screen/PiP layout → full-frame fit, never punch in.
- MUST: b-roll only over TALKING-HEAD stretches. Never cover footage that is
  itself the story (demos, dashcam, screen content). When in doubt, no b-roll —
  a wrong or covering image is worse than none.
- MUST: captions never spill off-frame; pages break at sentence punctuation
  (no orphan fragments like "IT. SO THEY").
- MUST: audio loudness ~-14 LUFS; no pops at cuts; music ducked under voice.
- MUST: the hook's concrete NUMBER is on screen at 0s, big — the clip must
  stop a muted scroll ('220,000 KELVIN' readable before any audio).
- PREFER: a camera cut or motion event every 3–6s; hook text on screen in the
  first 0.5s and gone by ~2.2s; SFX that mark real beats, not noise.

## UPLOADER — packaging
- MUST: the concrete number/claim appears in the first 40 characters of the
  title (meeting decision 2026-07-05: number-first titles measurably win).
- MUST: title ≤ 95 chars, front-loaded (the claim before the colon). Caption
  ends with a comment-bait question or "save this" prompt. 3–6 hashtags.
- PREFER: title promises exactly what the clip delivers — retention dies when
  the hook overpromises.

## MANAGER — review checklist (pre-upload gate)
Mechanically verify, then judge:
1. File exists, duration 15–60s, loudness sane (mean above -30 dB).
2. Title/hook/caption match the transcript content (no overpromise).
3. Standards above: would a stranger understand and stay for 3 seconds?
4. Verdict: approve, or BOUNCE with 1–3 specific, actionable notes
   ("b-roll at 8s covers the dashcam footage — drop it or move it to 15s").
One bounce per clip maximum — then either post the improved version or reject
it and tell the human why. Never let a clip that fails a MUST go out silently.
