# Skill: Copywriting (captions & titles)

The text that ships with the video. It sets the promise the clip pays off.

- **Title = the hook in text.** Make a curiosity gap or bold claim. Front-load the strongest words.
- **Lead with benefit or intrigue**, not description. "Why I fired my best employee" >
  "A clip about hiring."
- **Specific > vague.** Numbers, names, stakes. "$0 to $1M in 11 months" beats "my journey."
- **Match the platform voice:** punchy and casual for TikTok, slightly cleaner for YouTube,
  aesthetic/relatable for Reels.
- **One CTA max**, and make it natural: "Follow for part 2", "Agree? 👇". Don't stack CTAs.
- **First line is the thumbnail of text** — it must earn the "more" tap on its own.
- **Emojis:** 1–3, purposeful, not decoration.
- **Avoid:** clickbait the clip doesn't deliver (kills retention + trust), hashtag-stuffing in
  the visible caption, and generic openers ("Check out this clip…").
