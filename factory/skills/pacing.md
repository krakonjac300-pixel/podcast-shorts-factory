# Skill: Pacing & Rhythm

Pacing is the difference between "watched to the end" and "swiped at 4s."

- **Front-load value.** Hook in 0-3s, deliver something worthwhile by 5s. Never make the viewer
  wait for the windup. Even trimming 0.3s off the open measurably reshapes the retention cliff.
- **Visual velocity: something must change on screen every 1.5-2.0 seconds** (a cut, a punch
  zoom, a caption page turn, a number card, a reframe). Slower than 2.5s and attention drifts;
  faster than 1.2s reads as noise and gets tuned out. Cuts alone don't have to carry this:
  text swaps and zoom moves count.
- **Cut on the beat of speech**, not mid-thought. Trim the gaps between sentences hard;
  silence longer than ~0.5s is an exit signal.
- **Micro open-loops every 5-8s.** A tease, a numbered beat, a "here's where it gets worse"
  gives a fresh reason to stay between hook and payoff.
- **Pattern interrupt around 25-35s** on longer clips: a music drop, shot-size change, or a
  reframing caption resets attention right where drift typically starts.
- **Accelerate toward the payoff.** Shorter beats and rising energy as you approach the climax.
- **Vary the rhythm.** A few fast cuts, then a held beat for emphasis, then fast again. Monotone
  pacing (even fast) gets tuned out.
- **Strategic pause.** One deliberate silent beat before the punchline increases impact.
- **No dead ends.** Cut the clip the instant the payoff line lands. Trailing chatter kills the loop.
- **Target watch-through:** aim for the clip to feel ~20% shorter than viewers expect, leaving
  them slightly wanting more so they rewatch or check the next one.
- **Loop-friendly endings:** if the last frame can flow back into the first, completion and
  rewatch rates climb.
