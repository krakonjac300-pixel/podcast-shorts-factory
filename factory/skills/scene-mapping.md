# Skill: Scene Mapping (animation on value, rest on breathers)

How a high-level editor plans a talking-head short: go through the script LINE BY LINE
and ask ONE question per line — *is this line crucial for the viewer to understand?*

- **If crucial → it gets a visual.** A designed scene / animation that EXPLAINS the line.
  Visuals do exactly two jobs: (1) a viewer who SEES a concept understands it far better
  than with words alone, (2) every fresh visual resets attention so nobody swipes away.
- **If it's a breather → pure subtitles.** Between value peaks the viewer needs to rest
  and reconnect with the speaker's face. A breather section gets NOTHING on screen except
  captions — no b-roll, no memes, no inserts. Rest is a retention tool, not laziness.

Rhythm rules:
- Open on **pure subtitles for the hook** (~first 3s) so the viewer locks onto the speaker.
- Alternate: 1–2 visual scenes → one breather → visuals → breather. Never stack visuals
  wall-to-wall; never leave more than ~8s without either a visual or an emphasis moment.
- Lists in the script ("three habits…") are ONE visual that shows all items together.
- The final lesson/payoff line deserves a visual with the lesson written out on screen.

Design briefs (what each visual scene must specify):
- A CONCRETE subject that literally depicts the line — "person stepping out of a line of
  identical people, word EXTRAORDINARY above" not "success concept".
- On-screen TEXT of 1–4 words max, taken from the line itself.
- Stay ON BRAND: one consistent visual theme per channel (colors, minimal/realistic/paper
  style). Off-brand scenes make the edit feel pieced together and cheap.

Cost discipline (mock-up before motion):
- Design the STILL first, confirm it reads clearly at phone size, only then animate it.
  Animating is the expensive step; never animate an unproven design.
- Motion vocabulary that reads premium: slow constant zoom-in on the subject, text
  writing itself out, paper-tear/scale-in reveals. Constant gentle camera movement beats
  busy multi-directional motion (that's AI slop).

> The planner outputs a `scene_map` (visual vs breather segments with design briefs);
> the editor generates a branded design for each visual segment, animates it with a
> dynamic zoom, and keeps breather segments strictly captions-only.
