# Skill: Video Editing (short-form)

Turning a raw clip into a finished vertical short.

- **Frame the subject:** keep the speaker's eyes in the upper third. For 16:9 → 9:16, fit the
  speaker centered with a blurred fill behind, OR punch in so the face fills more of the frame.
- **Trim dead air:** cut breaths, "um/uh", false starts, and silences > 0.4s. Tight pacing
  raises retention. Jump-cuts on filler are expected and invisible in short-form.
- **Open on the hook frame** — never on a neutral resting face. Start on motion or expression.
- **Visual rhythm:** something should change on screen every 2–3 seconds (cut, zoom, caption
  emphasis, b-roll, graphic) to reset attention.
- **Captions are mandatory** — most viewers watch muted. Big, centered, word-synced.
- **Safe zones:** keep text clear of the top ~12% (platform UI) and bottom ~18% (caption/CTA UI).
- **Consistency:** same caption style, font, and intro treatment across a series builds a brand.
- **Export:** 1080×1920, H.264, yuv420p, ~8–12 Mbps, AAC audio. Loudness around -14 LUFS.

## 2026 research addendum (GitHub/industry sweep, 2026-07-17)
- **Crop to the ACTIVE SPEAKER, not the biggest face.** Pro auto-reframers (OpenShorts,
  Opus-class tools) track who is talking; our editor now does lips-motion active-speaker
  detection — trust it, and never frame a listener while someone else talks off-screen.
- **The 2.5-second law:** mobile attention for a static shot is ~2.8s. Something must
  change on screen every ≤2.5s: a cut, punch, caption pop, or emphasis color. Dynamic
  text + frequent cuts measure ~35% higher retention than static talking heads.
- **85% of short-form is watched MUTED.** Captions are the primary video track: bold,
  word-by-word pop, high-contrast highlight on the stake word. The audio is the bonus.
- **Clean beats busy.** Remove dead space and make each second intentional — but random
  overlays (stock photos, AI-art stills) over talking heads read as SLOP and get roasted.
  No decoration that competes with the speaker's face.
