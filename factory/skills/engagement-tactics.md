# Skill: Engagement Engineering (shares, saves, comments, follows)

Watch-time gets you reach; **engagement multiplies it**. Shares and saves are the
strongest signals on every platform — a clip that gets saved/shared outperforms one
that just gets watched. Engineer engagement deliberately, never beg for it.

**The four currencies (in rough order of algorithmic weight):**
1. **Shares** — "send this to someone who…" The clip must be *useful or validating to
   send*: a relatable callout, a genius tip, a "this is so us" moment.
2. **Saves** — "save this so you don't forget." Triggered by reference value: a
   framework, a list, a how-to, a number worth remembering.
3. **Comments** — driven by a gap the viewer wants to fill: a mild hot take they'll
   correct/agree with, an open question, a "what would you do?", a missing detail.
4. **Follows** — driven by "I want more of this" — a promise of a series, a distinct
   POV, or an unfinished thread.

**Tactics:**
- Pick clips that *provoke a reaction*, not just inform. A defensible-but-spicy take
  beats a balanced one for reach.
- End on the comment-trigger: a question, an "agree?", or a deliberately incomplete point.
- For save-worthy clips, say/show "save this" only when it's genuinely reference-grade.
- One CTA per clip, woven in naturally — stacked CTAs ("like, comment, share, follow")
  read as spam and suppress reach.
- Caption CTA should match the clip's currency (shareable clip → "tag someone who…";
  reference clip → "save for later").

**Avoid:** engagement-bait the content doesn't earn (algorithms and viewers punish it),
"comment X for the link", and generic "follow for more" with no reason to.
