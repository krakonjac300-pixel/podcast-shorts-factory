# Skill: Captions Craft

Captions are read, not decoration. They carry the clip for muted viewers.

- **Word-synced ("karaoke"):** show 1–4 words at a time and highlight the word as it's spoken.
  Full sentences sitting static are harder to track and lower retention.
- **Big and central:** large font, centered vertically (or lower-third), heavy weight, thick
  outline so it reads on any background.
- **High contrast:** white fill + black outline is the safe default. Highlight color (yellow,
  green) marks the active or emphasized word.
- **Emphasis:** enlarge / recolor the single most important word per line (the number, the
  verb, the surprise). Don't emphasize everything — then nothing stands out.
- **Punchy phrasing:** captions can tighten spoken filler ("you know", "kind of") without
  changing meaning. Match the spoken word for trust, but drop pure filler.
- **No wall of text:** never more than ~5 words on screen at once.
- **Readable timing:** a word group should stay long enough to read (~0.8s min) but track speech.
- **Accuracy matters:** a wrong auto-caption (esp. names/numbers) breaks trust — fix obvious errors.
