# Skill: Captions Craft

Captions are read, not decoration. They carry the clip for muted viewers, and 60-80% of
short-form viewers watch muted. Captions must be on screen from second 1, not fading in late.

- **Word-synced ("karaoke"):** show 1-4 words at a time and highlight the word as it's spoken.
  Full sentences sitting static are harder to track and lower retention.
- **Big and central:** large font, centered vertically (or lower-third), heavy weight, thick
  outline so it reads on any background. Sans-serif geometric faces measurably outperform
  lighter or serif faces on completion.
- **Safe zones (1080x1920):** keep all text inside the central 80% of width. Never in the
  bottom 20% (title/engagement UI), the top 10% (channel info), or the right 10-12%
  (like/comment column). Rule of thumb: caption baseline stays above y=1248px.
- **High contrast:** white fill + black outline is the safe default. Highlight color (yellow,
  green) marks the active or emphasized word. On busy footage, a semi-transparent dark bar
  behind the text beats outline alone.
- **Emphasis:** enlarge / recolor the single most important word per line (the number, the
  verb, the surprise). Don't emphasize everything, or nothing stands out.
- **Match the pace:** caption pages must track speech exactly; a lagging caption reads as a
  broken video. Fast clip = fast pages.
- **Punchy phrasing:** captions can tighten spoken filler ("you know", "kind of") without
  changing meaning. Match the spoken word for trust, but drop pure filler.
- **No wall of text:** never more than ~5 words on screen at once.
- **Readable timing:** a word group should stay long enough to read (~0.8s min) but track speech.
- **Accuracy matters:** a wrong auto-caption (esp. names/numbers) breaks trust; fix obvious errors.
