# Skill: Visual Effects & Motion

Motion keeps the eye locked. Subtle and purposeful beats flashy.

- **Punch-in zoom:** a quick 5–10% scale-up on an emphasized word or the payoff line adds
  energy and signals "this matters." Don't overuse — 1–3 per clip.
- **Word emphasis:** make the key word in a caption bigger / a different color as it's spoken.
  This is the cheapest, highest-impact effect in short-form. Pick the ONE word that carries the line.
- **Slow drift (Ken Burns):** a gentle continuous zoom over a static or talking-head shot keeps
  an otherwise still frame alive.
- **Transitions:** hard cut is the default. Use a whoosh-cut or quick zoom-transition only at a
  genuine scene/topic change — never decoratively. Avoid cheesy star-wipes.
- **Text reveals:** animate the hook title in (slide/pop) rather than a static drop.
- **B-roll / cutaways:** overlay a relevant image or clip when the speaker names a concrete
  thing (a place, product, number). Even 1–2 seconds breaks monotony.
- **Progress cues:** a subtle bar or word-count can boost completion on longer (45–60s) clips.
- **Restraint:** every effect must earn attention toward meaning. If it's just decoration, cut it.
