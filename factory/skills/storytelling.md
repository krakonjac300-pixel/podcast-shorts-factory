# Skill: Storytelling

Even a 25-second clip needs narrative shape, or it feels like a fragment.

- **Micro-arc:** setup → tension → payoff. Cut anything that isn't one of those three.
- **Open in motion.** Start as late into the story as possible (in medias res).
- **Tension before release.** Don't give the lesson until the audience feels the problem.
- **Specificity sells.** "$4,200 in March" lands; "a lot of money" doesn't. Keep concrete
  numbers, names, places that make it feel true.
- **Emotional throughline:** surprise, vindication, fear, awe, or humor. Name the emotion the
  clip should leave the viewer with, and protect it in the edit.
- **The turn:** the best clips have a moment where expectation flips. Find it and make sure
  it's intact and clearly audible.
- **Payoff = a clean, quotable line.** End on the sentence people would screenshot.
- **Cut ruthlessly:** filler words, tangents, repeated points, and anything before the hook or
  after the payoff. Tighter is almost always better.
