# Skill: Series & Loops (the compounding growth engine)

One-off clips get views; **series and loops compound them** into follows and rewatches.

**Loops — drive rewatches (a top ranking signal):**
- A clip that ends where it began makes the viewer watch twice without noticing → the
  algorithm sees >100% completion. Engineer it: the last line should set up the first line.
- Avoid hard stops and trailing dead air — they break the loop. Cut the instant the
  payoff lands so it flows back to the hook.
- Best for punchy, single-idea clips (a one-liner, a quick reveal).

**Series — drive follows + binge watch-time:**
- From a long podcast, identify a *thread* that can become multiple clips: "Part 1/2/3",
  "3 lessons" (one per clip), or a story split at a cliffhanger.
- End Part 1 on an open loop ("…and that's when it all went wrong — part 2") so the
  viewer follows/searches for the next. The follow is the goal, not just the view.
- Number them and keep a consistent hook/cover style so the series is recognizable.
- A guest's multi-point answer is a natural series — one clip per point.

**When scoring/selecting clips, flag:**
- Loopable moments (a line that could open AND close a clip).
- Series-able threads (a story or list that spans several clips) — propose the split.
- Cliffhanger cut points (where stopping mid-tension makes people want the rest).

**Avoid:** forcing a series where there's only one good moment; cliffhangers that feel
like withholding rather than genuine tension (viewers resent clickbait that doesn't pay off).
