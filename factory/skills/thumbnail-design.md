# Skill: Thumbnail / Cover Design

The cover frame is the single biggest lever on click-through for Reels & TikTok (and the
Shorts feed still shows a frame). Design it deliberately — don't let the platform pick.

- **Pick the most expressive frame:** wide eyes, open mouth mid-word, a laugh, a point. Never
  a neutral resting face or a blink. Emotion on the face = clicks.
- **3–5 huge words max.** One idea, readable at thumbnail size on a phone. "I WAS WRONG" beats
  a full sentence. Don't repeat the spoken hook verbatim — complement it.
- **Massive, bold, high-contrast text.** Heavy font, thick outline or a solid color bar behind
  it. White text + black stroke is the safe default; one accent color for the key word.
- **Text placement:** upper or lower third, never across the eyes. Keep it out of the bottom
  ~18% where the platform overlays the caption/UI.
- **Rule of one:** one face, one emotion, one line of text, one focal point. Clutter loses.
- **Series consistency:** same text style/position/color across clips makes your covers
  instantly recognizable in a feed — a free brand cue.
- **Contrast with the feed:** if everyone uses dark covers, a bright one stands out (and vice
  versa). Pattern-interrupt the scroll.
