# Skill: YouTube Guidelines & Monetization (survival rules for a clips channel)

A clips channel lives or dies by two YouTube rules: **copyright** (don't get struck) and
**"reused / inauthentic content"** (don't get demonetized). Follow these or the channel
gets no reach and no money.

## The reused-content rule (this is the big one, 2026)
YouTube's Partner Program **rejects channels that repost others' content without adding
substantial original value.** Renamed the "inauthentic content" policy (mid-2025):
low-effort, templated, mass-reposted clips are demonetized channel-wide.

**What makes a clip monetizable (do ALL of these — the pipeline already does):**
- **Substantive editing unique to the channel**: burned-in animated captions, 9:16 face-track
  reframe, sound-design/SFX, on-screen hook text, emphasis styling, trims.
- Add framing/context: a hook title and caption that reframe the moment as a takeaway.
- Keep a **consistent channel style** (same caption look, cover style) so it's clearly "yours."

**What gets you rejected/struck (never do):**
- Uploading a raw clip with just music slapped on, no edits.
- Ripping movies/TV/music or another creator's stream unchanged.
- Mass-producing near-identical low-effort uploads.

## Copyright (avoid strikes — 3 strikes = channel terminated)
- Clipping someone else's podcast is a **gray area**: expect possible **Content ID claims**
  (owner takes the revenue — common, not fatal) and occasionally **manual strikes** (serious).
- Lower the risk: keep clips short, transformative (captions/reframe/commentary), and don't
  clip music-heavy segments. Monitor the channel's "Copyright" tab; if a creator objects, stop.
- Safest long term: your **own** content, or creators who **allow/encourage** clips, or a
  licensing/affiliate arrangement.

## Monetization thresholds (2026)
- **Early access to YPP (fan funding, etc.):** 500 subs + 3 public uploads + 3,000 watch
  hours **or** 3M Shorts views (last 90 days).
- **Full ad revenue:** 1,000 subs + 4,000 public watch hours (12 mo) **or** 10M Shorts
  views (90 days).
- Shorts ad revenue is pooled/shared; **long-form watch hours pay more per view** — pair
  Shorts (discovery) with occasional long-form (revenue + watch hours).

## What this means for each agent
- **Finder:** pick moments that can be made genuinely transformative and are low copyright-risk.
- **Editor:** always apply the full edit stack (captions, reframe, SFX, hook, cover) — that
  transformation is what keeps the channel monetizable.
- **Uploader/Manager:** keep a consistent branded style; watch for claims; favor evergreen,
  advice/insight clips over music.
