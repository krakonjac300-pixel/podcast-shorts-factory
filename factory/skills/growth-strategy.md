# Skill: Growth Strategy — Views & Subscribers (2026 data-backed)

Concrete, current targets for turning clips into views and subscribers. These are the
numbers the 2026 Shorts algorithm actually rewards.

## Length: 30–45 seconds is the sweet spot
- **Sub-15s Shorts collapsed in reach** in 2026 — they can't clear the absolute watch-time
  bar even at 100% retention. Don't make ultra-short clips.
- **30–60s is where reach lives; 30–45s is the sweet spot.** Prefer clips in this band.

## Retention is the gate (this is THE metric)
- To get pushed wider, a Short needs roughly **≥50% retention (30–60s)** / ~65% (sub-30s),
  and **completed views + re-watches** are the only views that count meaningfully now.
- Structure every clip: **hook (0–1.5s) → setup (1.5–5s) → payoff.** Most weak clips die in
  the setup because it's too slow. State the value in the first 1.5 seconds.
- Engineer a **loop** (last line flows into the first) to drive re-watches.

## Engagement drives the test window
- **Comments are one of the strongest 2026 signals.** Every clip should end on a
  comment-trigger (a question, a mild hot take, "agree?"). Then **reply to the first ~50
  comments within 2 hours** of posting — this measurably boosts reach.
- Shares and saves matter too (see engagement-tactics).

## Posting cadence
- **12+ uploads/month → ~66% more subscribers and ~53% more views** than 1–3/month.
  The 3-posts-a-day schedule (≈90/month) is well above the growth threshold.
- But **satisfaction-per-video beats raw volume** — never ship a weak clip just to hit a
  number. One strong clip > five mediocre ones.

## The subscriber engine
- **~75% of Shorts views come from non-subscribers** — Shorts are a *discovery/top-of-funnel*
  tool. The job of a Short is to earn the follow, not just the view.
- **Channels posting Shorts + long-form grow subscribers ~3× faster.** Occasionally post a
  longer compilation/best-of to convert Short viewers into subscribers and bank watch hours.
- Give every clip a reason to subscribe: a distinct POV, a recurring series ("Part 2"),
  or an open loop (see series-and-loops).

## Small-channel edge (2026)
- There's an **original-sound bonus for channels under 50K** — using original voiceover/audio
  gives a measurable lift. Podcast-voice clips qualify; don't bury them under loud music.

## Niche consistency
- Pick a lane and stay in it (money/finance, health, mindset, business…). The algorithm needs
  a consistent audience signal to know who to show you to. Whiplashing topics slows growth.
