# Skill: Marketing Psychology

Why people stop, watch, and share.

- **Pattern interrupt:** the brain ignores the expected. Open with something that violates the
  scroll's rhythm — an odd claim, a hard cut, a strong visual.
- **Open loops (Zeigarnik effect):** an unfinished thought nags until resolved. Promise a payoff
  early ("the third one shocked me") and only deliver at the end.
- **Identity & in-group:** "If you're an X, you already know…" makes the right viewer lean in
  and share to signal belonging.
- **Loss aversion:** "what you're losing by not doing this" outperforms "what you'd gain."
- **Social proof & specificity:** concrete results and numbers feel verifiable and credible.
- **Emotional contagion:** the emotion the speaker shows is the emotion the viewer catches —
  protect genuine surprise, conviction, or laughter in the cut.
- **Sharability triggers:** people share what makes them look smart, in-the-know, or like a
  good friend ("you need to see this"). Engineer one of those into the payoff.
- **Cognitive ease:** simple words, short sentences, clear captions. Confusion = swipe.
