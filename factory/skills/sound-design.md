# Skill: Sound Design & Sound Effects

Audio is half the experience and the most underused lever in clips.

- **Voice first.** Music and SFX must never bury speech. Duck background music to ~10–15% under
  dialogue; bring it up only in pauses or at the outro.
- **Music choice sets emotion:** tense underscore for stories, upbeat for energy, lo-fi/none for
  intimate confessions. Match the clip's emotional throughline, don't default to hype.
- **SFX as punctuation** (use sparingly, 2–4 per clip max):
  - *whoosh* on a hard cut or scene change
  - *riser* building into the payoff line
  - *impact/boom* on the mic-drop or big reveal
  - *ding/pop* when a key word or number appears on screen
  - *record-scratch* on a "wait, what?" beat
- **Silence is a tool.** A beat of total silence right before the punchline makes it hit harder.
- **Loudness:** normalize so the clip isn't quieter than the platform's other content (-14 LUFS).
- **Clean up:** remove loud plosives/clicks; high-pass voice ~80Hz to cut rumble.

> The editor exports recommended SFX cues (type + timestamp) into the clip's notes sidecar so
> they can be added in a finishing pass. Music is mixed in automatically.
